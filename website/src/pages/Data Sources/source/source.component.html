The High-Resolution Rapid Refresh (HRRR) is an advanced operational weather prediction system developed by the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA). Here's a summary of its key features:

  • Real-Time, High-Resolution Model: The HRRR is a real-time atmospheric model with a 3-km resolution. It updates hourly, offering detailed and timely weather predictions​​.
  • Numerical Forecast Model and Analysis/Assimilation System: It comprises a numerical forecast model coupled with an analysis/assimilation system that initializes the model. This system ensures accurate and up-to-date weather predictions​​.
  • Geographical Coverage: The HRRR covers the conterminous United States and Alaska. For the contiguous U.S., it updates hourly, while for Alaska, it updates every three hours​​.
  • Vertical Levels and Model Implementation: The model operates with 50 vertical levels, providing detailed atmospheric data from the surface up through the atmosphere. It is an implementation of the Advanced Research version of the Weather Research and Forecast (WRF-ARW) Model, specifically designed to allow convection processes within its predictions​​​​.
  • Specialized Applications for Fire and Smoke Prediction: One of the notable applications of HRRR is its capability to ingest fire radiative power data from polar-orbiting NOAA and NASA satellites. This feature, known as HRRR-smoke, is used to calculate fire size, biomass burning emissions, and predict the height and spread of smoke plumes. Originally experimental, this capability is now a standard part of the operational HRRR system
